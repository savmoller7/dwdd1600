Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson was the main author of the Declaration of Independence and later became the third President of the United States. He believed strongly in individual rights and limited government.
John Adams
John Adams was a leader in the movement for independence and helped persuade Congress to break away from Britain. He later became the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin was a famous inventor, writer, and diplomat who helped gain French support for the American Revolution. He was one of the most respected figures in the colonies.
John Hancock
John Hancock was the president of the Second Continental Congress and is best known for his large, bold signature on the Declaration of Independence.
Samuel Adams
Samuel Adams was a strong political organizer and revolutionary leader who helped rally colonists against British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
Richard Henry Lee was the man who formally proposed independence to the Continental Congress in 1776, leading directly to the writing of the Declaration of Independence.
Roger Sherman
Roger Sherman was a statesman who helped shape early American government and was the only person to sign all four major founding documents.
Robert Morris
Robert Morris was a wealthy merchant who helped finance the American Revolution, providing crucial support to the Continental Army.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
