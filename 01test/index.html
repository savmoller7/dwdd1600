Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship means using technology and the internet responsibly, safely, and ethically. It's about how people behave online and how they understand their rights and responsibilities in digital spaces.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the rules, regulations, and legal responsibilities that govern how people use technology, the internet, and digital content.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy. An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules that explain how technology and internet resources may and may not be used.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's work, ideas, or words without giving proper credit and presenting them as your own.

Plagiarism is copying or using another person's work or ideas without crediting the original source.

What is Academic Dishonesty

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their own learning.

Academic dishonesty is any form of cheating, plagiarism, or deception in schoolwork or assessments.

What Are the Rules for AI Generated Content?

The rules for AI-generated content explain when and how AI tools (like ChatGPT or image generators) may be used for schoolwork without violating academic integrity.
